**ACT I: THE MASKS WE WEAR**

Jules Rivera seems ordinary at Westbridge Elite—quiet, compliant, and easily overlooked by classmates who value tradition above self-expression. But beneath crisp uniforms and calculated smiles, Jules is a non-binary ballet prodigy, keeping both their identity and wildest dreams carefully concealed from their conservative parents and pressure-cooker academic life. Each night, they come alive as “Juliette,” dazzling crowds at secret, glittering drag balls deep in the city’s midnight heart.

The duality thrills and exhausts Jules, who finds solace among a vibrant circle of underground performers: fiery best friend Sasha, nurturing MC Queen Dominique, and enigmatic, charismatic newcomer, Ash. With each clandestine performance, Jules tastes freedom—and falls a little more in love with both the stage and Ash’s fearless confidence. But the threat of being outed looms large; exposure could cost them everything, from their ballet scholarship to even their home.

On the night of the season’s most anticipated drag event, Jules stuns the crowd with a daring routine…but disaster strikes. In a sudden, shocking act of sabotage, stage lights crash to the floor, nearly injuring Jules and throwing the show into chaos. The glittering ballroom now echoes with suspicion, and whispers target Jules as the prime suspect—someone desperate for the spotlight, someone with something to prove.

---

**ACT II: UNDER THE SPOTLIGHT’S GLARE**

With the police circling and the drag community divided, Jules launches their own investigation to clear their name. In the labyrinthine underworld of the ballroom scene, trust fractures: old rivalries flare and new secrets come to light. Sasha grows distant, torn between loyalty and fear of being implicated. Ash is at Jules’ side, pushing them toward vulnerability—and challenging them to imagine a world where they can truly be themselves.

As rumors swirl at school, Jules juggles mounting anxiety, deteriorating grades, and the ever-present risk of both being outed and losing their hard-won place on the ballet squad. The weight of suspicion fuels Jules’ growing resolve: they are tired of hiding, tired of shame. Betrayed by someone close and realizing the sabotage may not have been about them at all, Jules must confront chilling truths about jealousy, insecurity, and the cost of denying one’s identity.

Through heartbreak, stolen moments with Ash, and the fierce support of found family, Jules redefines what heroism means. Challenged during a second, even riskier performance, Jules dances not as a disguise but as their authentic self—risking exposure to save the community who truly knows them.

---

**ACT III: DANCING IN THE LIGHT**

With everything on the line, Jules pieces together damning clues, piecing together a truth no one expected: the saboteur is a performer Jules once considered a friend, driven by jealousy and desperation. The shocking confrontation forces Jules to choose between revenge and forgiveness—between returning to the shadows or stepping boldly into the light.

News of the sabotage and Jules’s secret breaks wider than expected. At school, whispers become shouts; at home, their world shudders. Yet as the dust settles, something unexpected happens: stung by betrayal but buoyed by Ash’s love and the drag family’s unwavering support, Jules discovers strength in vulnerability. For the first time, they stand in the spotlight—unmasked, unapologetic, and authentic.

The story closes as Jules prepares to dance in a public showcase, no longer hiding who they are, galvanizing others to embrace their own truths. Though the consequences are real and some wounds slow to heal, the message is electric: the courage to shine, even when the world demands conformity, can inspire a revolution—one stiletto step at a time.

# THE NOVEL


## Chapter 1: Behind the Velvet Curtain


The chill scraped its way up my bare arms the second I slipped inside the backstage corridor of the Moonlight Ballroom, the battered duffel bag biting into my palm. My breath came hard, curling in the sharp air like I’d run six blocks instead of just crossing from a shadowed alley and up peeling fire stairs, heels hidden deep in my bag. Every footfall echoed—too loud—but if anyone noticed, nobody said a thing. I pressed on, skirting past rails of abandoned gowns, stacks of wigs staring with silent, foam-faced judgment. I found the cracked mirror in the far corner and claimed it with a silent, defiant dare: this was my space now. I dropped the bag and slid onto the metal chair, cold biting beneath the thin layer of tights. My hands shook as I unzipped, laying out the tools—foundation stick worn to the nub, palette smeared with powder, cracked tube of cherry-red. The mirror’s flickering light showed every bruise, every old shadow beneath my eyes. I stared at myself—Jules, schoolboy ghost, too-soft around the edges for home or ballet—and forced my hand steady. Foundation blurred freckles, smoothed jaw. Highlighter blazed my cheekbones into something new, fierce. My heart rattled like a maraca in my ribs, the bassline from the ballroom a faint pulse, teasing through the walls. Laughter floated up—a shriek, a chorus of cackles. The drag queens were prepping, louder than the sea. I tucked my curls beneath a nylon cap. A flick of liner, thick lashes glued with trembling fingers. As I finished, Juliette began shimmering into shape: cheekbones sharp as secrets, glitter dusted across eyelids. Every practiced motion—brush, dab, blend—drove the dread further down, made room for something sweeter. Not gone, but muffled. My chest squeezed from the fear of discovery. What if Sasha followed me tonight? Or worse—my parents, hard faces and harder questions, the ones I never answered. I curled hands in my lap, pushing memories aside. Becoming Juliette was the closest I came to truly breathing. Beneath the clatter and sharp chill, I made myself gleam. Each shimmer, a little mercy.




## Chapter 2: Pressed Under Spotlight


The noise of Westbridge Elite spun around me in jagged gusts—slamming lockers, sneakers squeaking, a hurricane of bodies moving on practiced autopilot. My head bowed, chin nearly tacked to my sternum, I slipped between the tide, backpack strap digging into my collarbone. Nothing stung worse than being invisible and, somehow, still noticed. Whispers tumbled after me in crisp syllables, bitten off just loud enough. "Is that—?" "Check the liner, what a joke." My breath buckled at the edges, fingers curling tighter. Did they see? The flick of pink I'd tried to smudge away with a tissue before homeroom, a too-bright echo of the night before — my real self, not sanctioned, not safe here. Everything about me at this school felt like an accident waiting to be exposed.

At my locker, the world blurred for a moment—a swirl of scuffed shoes and navy wool. Then two silhouettes closed in behind me, their grins crisp and carnivorous. Eliza leaned into her friend, gaze pinning my reflection in the cheap locker mirror. "Sweet glasses, Jules," she murmured, red nails grazing the side of her mouth, lips curled. A quick flick of her eyes, landing square on my eyelids. "Planning to join the cheer team? Or are you auditioning for clown college?" Her laugh was sharp, brittle. I hunched further, palms slick, fingers fumbling with the dial. My heart stuttered. If I made eye contact, they'd win. So I stared at the textbooks like they could save me, practiced making myself smaller still.

Behind me, they snickered, echo bright as glass in sunlight. I grabbed my French reader, slammed the locker shut maybe too hard. Someone down the hall barked, "Careful! Watch it, Rivera," as if even my silence could provoke irritation. Always too much or not enough. I slipped away, heat blooming up my neck. No escape, really. Every glance felt like a verdict, every word another pin pressed into soft skin. Inside, I counted: Four hours until rehearsal. A dozen more until I could breathe. I slid past the trophy case—a wall of perfect smiles, all shining under glass. Not one of them mine.




## Chapter 3: Gilded Sanctuary


The sharp, sweet scent of hairspray hung thicker than the velvet curtains, blending with the crackle of energy swelling behind the runway. Even before I ducked past the gold-tassled entrance, the ballroom’s heart-thrum of bass leapt into my chest—steady, insistent, promising escape. Sequins flashed in the mirrors, queens half-made coiling and stretching in slivers of blue and pink light. I lingered in the shadow by the stage door, pulse yammering: one half of me jonesing to melt into shadow, the other snapping its teeth for approval. That’s when Sasha streaked through a spill of yellow stage light, all torn fishnets and swagger, hollering, “JULES!” like I’d been gone for years. She crushed me to her ribcage in a hug that squashed every drop of panic, her arms solid and grippy as a wrestler. I laughed into her shoulder, muffled and startled, as her hair tickled my cheek. “Thought you were ghosting us, Rivera. Don’t think I wouldn’t drag your haunted ass out of hiding,” she barked, winking at the twins powdering false lashes nearby. With a flourish, she spun her silver ring—the one I’d given her the night we swore this place was home—off her thumb. She held it out, palm up, trembling minutely. “Here. You’re on duty.” Her voice softened, almost lost under the crowd’s warm murmur. “Protection spell. For nerves. For you.” I grinned, shaky, let her slip it onto my finger. It flashed bright against my skin, cool and somehow heavier than before. The other queens whooped, clapped, their laughter fraying the edges of my tension. “Watch out!” someone crowed. “Jules got the power. That’s cheating!” Sasha pressed her forehead against mine, low and silly: “‘By the ancient laws of Sparkle and Duct Tape, you are transformed,’” she intoned, drawing the words out like a royal decree. But underneath her bravado, I saw it—something uncertain flickering in her eyes, as if she needed to believe, too. The ring glinted fierce and defiant. I squeezed her hand, the metal biting cold in my palm, and tried to swallow the thought that it was all too good, too fragile—a little magic never stopped the real monsters from finding us.




## Chapter 4: A House Divided


The Rivera dining room always felt too small on nights like this—the air heavy with the clatter of silverware and my mother’s starched tablecloth, everything just a shade too proper. My father still wore his work tie, loosened just enough to signal the end of the day but not enough to invite real comfort. I kept my posture perfect, hands folded in my lap, feeling the neat press of my uniform skirt against my knees, a costume I’d learned by rote. 

"Julián." My mother’s voice cut the silence, crisp as the rim of her water glass. "Your grades have always been a point of pride. Your last report—" She ran a manicured finger down the edge of a printed email by her plate. "There have been slips. Assignments missing. This isn’t like you."

Sasha’s fork clinked against her plate, too loud, but she kept her head down, slicing chicken into geometric pieces. She pressed her thumb to the old silver ring I gave her last spring, spinning it absently. She was trying not to look at me, but I could feel her attention, as sharp as the prick of a pin.

My father’s sigh was measured, a quiet censure. "Ballet, school—these are responsibilities, hijo. Your teachers write that you missed practice. You promised you would make us proud."

I nodded, practiced, careful. "I’m just tired. Some nights, homework piles up. Maybe I lost track."

The lie stuck to my tongue and lodged there, dry and heavy. I could feel my mother’s disappointment press against my ribcage, even as her words stayed shaped by concern. "Julián, you know the scholarship depends on your commitment. And college—" Her eyes flickered to Sasha, then back to me. "We expect better. This family expects better. Do you understand?"

There was no air left. Only the tick of the old kitchen clock, Sasha’s leg bouncing under the table, and the sudden rawness behind my eyes. "Of course," I said, steady and flat, as if good posture could keep me from unraveling right there. "I’ll do better."

Sasha flipped her hair forward, chewing at her lip, pale skin gone blotchy around the cheeks the way it always did when stress hit her too hard. Nobody mentioned her late-night absences, the teachers’ calls to her mother. All eyes were on me.

My father nodded, final, like a judge. "We work so hard for you. Don’t let distractions get in the way."

I bit the inside of my cheek, hard, and tried to build a wall—between me and their expectations, between the neatness of this room and who I had been just last night, knees bruised from hours on a different kind of stage, sequined lashes prickling at the corner of my eyes. I wondered, looking at Sasha’s reflection in the old glass cabinet, if she saw through the blank calm on my face, if she could tell how close the shame was to breaking through.

"May I be excused?" My voice sounded too soft—almost someone else’s.

My mother’s frown deepened, but she nodded. I stood, feeling as if the invisible leash of their rules was digging into my skin, and caught Sasha glancing at me, mouth pressed thin.

If she said anything now, the house would collapse. Instead, she just spun her ring once, reluctance—or regret—flashing in her eyes. I walked out, the ache of their disappointment bowing my spine. The distance between the life they wanted and the one I lived felt like a chasm I might never cross.




## Chapter 5: Sabotage in Sequins


I slipped through the velvet-draped doors before the city outside was even shaking off last night’s rain. The ballroom was a cave of anticipation: mirrored tiles winking with promise, cables sprawled like nervous veins underfoot. I sank into Juliette—wig perched high and frosted, sequins scaling up my spine, battered pointe shoes invisible beneath the hem. Backstage throbbed with the kind of chaos that had become ritual. Dominique swept past, regal in wine-red velvet, her booming laughter cutting through the fray as queens spat jokes and glued lashes. The air tasted of burnt hair and foundation dust.

Sasha flitted toward me, eyeliner uneven, hands fluttery as moths as she pressed a glittery lash to my cheekbone. "Don’t blink yet. We don’t have to call the paramedics this early," she muttered, but her eyes skittered away from mine. There was too much energy under her skin tonight; the kind that comes before storms, not parties. I tried to laugh but it snagged in my throat.

Ash sidled in behind, trailing sharp clove and violet. They pressed a hand to the swirl of tulle at my hip. "You’re about to break hearts, Rivera," they said, voice low. Their compliment hit me between ribs and spine, steadied the stitches in my heart. I wanted to pocket the sound of my name in Ash's mouth—something clean, seen, real.

Dominique clapped her hands, summoning us close. We curled together—a kaleidoscope of rhinestones, sweat, ringed fingers gripping tighter than stage fright. Her eyes were honey-dark as she locked us in. "Remember, loves," she chimed, velvet draping every word, "the world waits to see you fall—it’s up to you whether you soar instead." A pulse leapt in my throat. For a second, our house was unbreakable, every bruise and setback polished until it glittered. But even as we cheered and hugged, a chill slipped in beside me: Sasha’s laughter came out brittle, Dominique’s warning heavy as a lock around my chest. Maybe the world’s glare could reach even here.




## Chapter 6: Wounds Exposed

## Chapter 16: Broken Chains

## Chapter 6: Wounds Exposed

## Chapter 11: Rebelling in Satin


A hush fell over the rooftop when the heavy door swung inward and then closed with a gentle snap. I nodded. The city was loud out here, but inside Dominique’s steady grip, there was a quiet that made it easier to breathe. My hand curled tighter around the jacket. 'I’ll keep them safe,' I said, and almost believed it. She let me go, her touch a silent promise, her shadow still stretching behind me as I went back to the others, trying hard to keep my hands from shaking with the weight of what she’d given me.




## Chapter 12: The Spotlight’s Edge


It happened on the beat between breaths—a trembling hinge of possibility. ## Chapter 13: Shattered Mirrors


I sat alone on the worn marley floor, knees drawn to my chest, in the city’s echoing night studio. Ash slid to the other side, back pressed to the damaged mirror; their perfume curled between us, spicy and impossible to hide.

“You trying to become a ghost, Jules?” Ash’s tone was half-joke, half-lament. I managed a crooked smile that only ached.

I closed my eyes, swallowing old shame. The studio lights buzzed low. Dominique’s hand found mine, warm and steady, her rings sharp against my skin. For the first time in days, I let myself wish for something kinder, even as fear chewed at my ribs. “If I burn down what’s left—my family, my chance at school—what’s left?”

Dominique didn’t hesitate. “What’s left is you. And if you trust us, that’s family, too.”

I didn’t trust myself to speak. But some part of me—trembling, ruined—leaned closer, holding on to both their hands. For a second, I almost felt real.

Dominique’s presence snapped into focus—her velvet robes sweeping across the floor, the slow clack of her heels demanding silence. ## Chapter 15: Revelations in the Light


Spotlights bruised the ceiling, sweeping across a masquerade of masks and faces painted in every color shimmering under gold. My mother sobbed softly, more out of terror than grief. My father ranted about disgrace and consequences, his voice rising like a whip. I pressed my hands to the battered pointe shoes, the familiar curve grounding me. For a second I was still, pulled taut between all my worlds—and then something inside me flamed raw.

You say you love me, but you don't even know me—you love someone I was pretending to be. All because you're afraid of gossip? I'd rather be a freak than keep dying in here!”

I care about breathing, about not wanting to disappear every time I see myself in the mirror. I am Juliette. I’m still Jules. It’s all me. I can’t hide anymore.”

My father’s mouth twisted as if he’d been slapped. My mother’s sobs splintered. Threats hung between us—expulsion, grounding, worse—but I wouldn’t let them stick this time. The pain found edges inside me, but for once it carved out room for defiance instead of shame.

For the first time, I walked out into the street with my head held high. The night stung cold, the doors closing behind me like a warning—but something had shifted. The world saw me now, all of me, burning and bare. The price hurt like hell. But shame had no place left to hide.




## Chapter 13: Shattered Mirrors


My shoes clicked alone across the floor, echoes pinballing off the shadows that stacked against the empty ballroom’s high windows. I stood in the hush, glass fragment cold in my pocket, and drew out my phone with shaking hands, punch-drunk on adrenaline and exhaustion alike. My reflection loomed just visible in a stray mirror panel—eyes red, cheeks streaked, something hard and old finally eroding.

Voice mail. Dominique. I didn’t give myself time to think, only breathed in the tang of dust, copper of glitter, the sweet-sour of old fear. When the beep sounded, my words tumbled out hoarse:

I let the silence linger, my own breath stuttering on the recording. Then hung up. The phone felt impossible to hold—heavy as the truth, small as hope. All I could do was stand there alone, the ballroom’s dead lights flickering gold above me, and wait for whatever came crashing next.




## Chapter 14: Pieces of Truth

The music shifted, a swell trembling through the chandeliers. People gathered toward the main stage, hush falling like snow. Dominique’s voice rang out—velvet and iron—setting the battle lines. Ash squeezed my shoulder. Sasha gritted her teeth. Across the room, Emery straightened, the earring glinting pale against her palm.

I drew in a breath laced with nerves and sugar and fear. This was it—my line in the sand, my truth no longer hidden under layers of costume and shadow. My legs felt hollow with dread, but the crowd’s silent hunger for spectacle pressed me onward.

I stepped out under the lights, costume dazzling. The room held its breath—the city, the family I found, the friend I lost. I wasn’t just about to reveal a saboteur. As the applause thundered and the stage beckoned, I understood for the first time what bravery felt like—the terrifying, beautiful kind born from being seen, whole and hurting. I braced myself, heart full and raw, ready to call Emery’s name, ready to risk everything for the truth.

## Chapter 16: Broken Chains


The tension in the city pulsed like a thunderstorm too slow to break—endless rumors swirling, old teachers with forced smiles, but then a ripple of music echoed beneath the school’s stone archway. ## Chapter 17: In Their Own Light


The last pose hung in the air—a slash of arm, a reach beyond my own doubt—and for a second everything in the theater went utterly still. No breathing, no cough, not even a rustle of dress in the aisle. The silence trembled, stretched, filled with possibility and terror.
