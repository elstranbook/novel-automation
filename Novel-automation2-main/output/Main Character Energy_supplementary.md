# Supplementary Materials: Main Character Energy

## Premises & Endings

### Chosen Premise

A socially awkward teen discovers a magical vintage camera that gives her 'main character energy,' altering how others see her—and herself—forcing her to balance confidence with authenticity.

### All Premises

1. A socially awkward teen discovers a magical vintage camera that gives her 'main character energy,' altering how others see her—and herself—forcing her to balance confidence with authenticity.

2. After a mysterious online app grants her the ability to project confidence and charm, a shy teen learns the cost of altering her persona to fit in, questioning who she really is.

3. A quiet bookworm accidentally unleashes 'main character energy' during a school play, gaining attention and influence but struggling to keep her true self amid the spotlight.

4. When a socially anxious teen receives personalized advice from an enigmatic stranger that makes her irresistibly charismatic, she must decide whether to keep following the script or rewrite her story.

5. A teen with a reputation for being invisible wakes up one day with the power to shape how people perceive her, learning that true confidence doesn’t come from changing others’ perspectives but embracing her quirks.

6. After a chance encounter with a street artist, a reserved girl gains a newfound allure that draws people in, challenging her to unite her shy personality with a vibrant persona without losing herself.

7. In a world obsessed with social media perfection, a teenager’s ability to command attention leads her on a journey to distinguish genuine self-love from external validation.

8. A teen who’s always blended into the background gains ‘main character energy’ after a heartfelt confession, compelling her to explore love, friendship, and the courage to be seen authentically.

9. After finding a secret journal that lets her rewrite moments of her life, a shy girl uses 'main character energy' to enhance her social status but soon discovers that true growth requires embracing imperfections.

10. A loner with a sharp wit discovers that her words can shape not only conversations but the way people see her, forcing her to face the dilemma between staying true and becoming who others expect.


### Chosen Ending

The camera’s magic fades, but she discovers that the confidence it sparked has become internalized, empowering her even without supernatural help.

### All Potential Endings

1. She decides to give up the magical camera, realizing her authentic self is enough, and starts using ordinary photos to document her genuine journey with newfound confidence.

2. Her 'main character energy' inadvertently causes a rift with her closest friends, but through honest conversations, they accept her true self, strengthening their bond.

3. She uses the camera one last time to create a project highlighting everyone's unique stories, embracing individuality over conformity.

4. The camera’s magic fades, but she discovers that the confidence it sparked has become internalized, empowering her even without supernatural help.

5. She confronts the source of the camera’s magic and learns it was a reflection of her own untapped potential all along, leading to a breakthrough in self-acceptance.

6. Her popularity peaks but leaves her feeling empty; ultimately, she chooses meaningful connections over social status, growing into a leader who celebrates differences.

7. She accidentally exposes the camera’s magic to the whole school, sparking a movement where students embrace their flaws and reject social pressures.

8. She loses the camera but finds a hobby—photography—that helps her express herself creatively and boosts her self-worth.

9. She uses the camera to help a shy classmate gain confidence, learning that uplifting others is a true source of empowerment.

10. After a romantic interest sees her without the camera’s influence and accepts her authentic self, she realizes that self-worth comes from being loved for who she really is.


## Novel Plan

Certainly! Below is a comprehensive three-part novel plan for *Main Character Energy* designed to guide the crafting of a compelling, emotionally rich narrative across approximately 110,800 words. Each section includes key milestones, explicit character development steps, conflict arcs, secondary character dynamics, thematic weaving, pacing advice, and foreshadowing/payoff strategies.

# **Overall Novel Word Count:** ~110,800 words  
- Act I (The Awakening): ~35,000 words (~32%)  
- Act II (The Conflict): ~45,000 words (~41%)  
- Act III (The Resolution): ~30,800 words (~27%)

---

# **PART I: THE AWAKENING** (~0-35,000 words)

### Key Milestones & Plot Points:
- Introduce Avery’s everyday “invisible” life and internal world: school, record store job, sketchbook, Lila, Lucas from afar.  
- Establish Avery’s core desires (to be seen authentically) and fears (invisibility).  
- Discovery of the vintage Polaroid camera, initial magical moment, and spark of ‘main character energy.’  
- The first shifts: Avery starts acting differently, sparks notice from teachers, peers, and especially Lucas.  
- Moments of exhilaration balancing with the first hints of internal tension as Avery tries on this new identity.  
- Tension emerges in relationship with Lila: feelings of abandonment start subtly.  
- Close Act I with a significant decision or event where Avery actively chooses to embrace this new power, setting up the tension between authentic self and crafted persona.

### Approximate Word Count: 30000-35000

---

### 1. **Character Development (Avery)**

- **Arc Beginning:** Present Avery as shy, observant, tentative. Her quiet habits (sketching, low-key style) show her authentic self, valued internally but underappreciated externally.  
- **Emotional Beats:** Capture Avery’s yearning through internal monologue and subtle dialogue (e.g., late-night talks with Lila).  
- **Transformation Moment:** Use the magic camera as a literal and metaphorical catalyst—describe vividly the emotional rush when she feels seen and heard for the first time.  
- **Key Decision:** Avery’s choice to use the camera again, signaling tentative acceptance of this new persona.

---

### 2. **Conflict & Tension**

- **Central Conflict Introduced:** Avery’s internal struggle between her authentic shy self and the more magnetic persona the camera creates.  
- **Tension Balance:** Alternate scenes of thrilling social acceptance with quieter, reflective moments that reveal Avery’s inner doubts—bedroom sketching sessions that contrast sharply with school popularity.  
- **Social Dynamics:** Establish social cliques and pressures at school to contextualize Avery’s insecurity and temptation.

---

### 3. **Secondary Characters**

- **Lila:** Introduced as Avery’s grounded, honest counterpoint. Show their strong bond and early signs of strain as Avery begins changing. Use natural, emotionally charged dialogue that hints at Lila’s concern.  
- **Lucas:** A mostly observed figure at first, representing Avery’s romantic longing. His casual presence and moments of glance create tension and hope.  
- **Other Peers:** Develop minor social figures around school as a background chorus—show different reactions to Avery’s emerging persona to reflect social complexity.

---

### 4. **Themes**

- **Visibility vs. Authenticity:** Weave these naturalistically through Avery’s reflections and dialogue. Symbolism: the camera (external perception), the sketchbook (internal reality).  
- **Identity & Transformation:** Focus on the allure and danger of “becoming someone else.” Use early foreshadowing hints (e.g., the camera’s inscription/metaphorical notes).  
- **Friendship & Trust:** Begin planting the seeds of tension in Avery-Lila’s dynamic.

---

### 5. **Pacing & Structure**

- Start deliberately slow to establish immersion in Avery’s inner life. Use detailed, sensory-rich descriptions.  
- Introduce the magical element early enough to shift narrative momentum at ~20% mark.  
- Embed a “cliffhanger” at Act I’s end where Avery publicly embraces the camera's magic with joyous yet ambiguous consequences (e.g., a social success tempered by an uneasy feeling).  
- Alternate quiet, introspective scenes with social “highs” to maintain emotional variability and prevent monotony.

---

### 6. **Foreshadowing & Payoff**

- Introduce subtle hints that suggest the camera’s power is double-edged (maybe it flickers briefly).  
- Early tensions between Avery and Lila hint at eventual alienation.  
- Lucas’s ambiguous interest foreshadows later romantic conflict.  
- Avery’s struggle with self-worth seeded in her internal narrative.

---

# **PART II: THE CONFLICT** (~35,000-80,000 words)

### Key Milestones & Plot Points:
- Avery’s social rise continues but increasingly feels forced and performative.  
- Strain and distancing from Lila escalate into confrontations.  
- Lucas’s growing interest causes tension as he is attracted to the ‘energized’ Avery, not the real one.  
- Other students begin imitating Avery’s ‘main character energy,’ diluting its novelty and authenticity and increasing social tensions.  
- Avery’s anxiety festers; she starts to fatigue under the pressure of maintaining the façade.  
- Magic of the camera becomes unstable—unpredictable flashes symbolize Avery’s emotional fragility.  
- Pivotal public breaking point: Avery’s image cracks publicly (e.g., embarrassing social fallout, a failed event, or confrontation).  
- Act II closes on Avery’s moment of emotional vulnerability and crisis, deciding whether to cling to illusion or face the truth.

### Approximate Word Count: 45,000

---

### 1. **Character Development (Avery)**

- **Emerging Fractures:** Delve deeply into Avery’s internal conflict—use internal monologue and brief, sharp dialogues revealing her anxiety and sense of losing self.  
- **Transformation:** Shift from euphoria to exhaustion; showcase emotional scenes where Avery questions her worth outside the camera’s influence.  
- **Key Decisions:** Moments where Avery chooses between the façade and vulnerability (e.g., rejecting or accepting Lila’s confrontation, scolding herself after social failure).  
- **Growth Moments:** Avery’s recognition of the limits of popularity and superficial connection.

---

### 2. **Conflict & Tension**

- Escalate tensions deftly: social pressure, romantic confusion, and deteriorating friendship all intertwine.  
- Contrast high-stakes social scenes (party, school event, confrontation) with quieter moments where Avery retreats to sketching or solo reflection.  
- Use pacing to compress tension near the climax of Act II, with shorter scenes or chapters increasing narrative urgency.

---

### 3. **Secondary Characters**

- **Lila:** Take a more active role—she confronts Avery, challenges her choices, threatens the friendship yet desires reconciliation. Lila’s mini-arc involves softening in patience, learning to listen even as she holds firm.  
- **Lucas:** Show growing confusion and frustration—he starts noticing inconsistencies, triggering subtle emotional clashes. Reveal his own insecurities in conversations that illuminate his vulnerability.  
- **Peers:** Include foils—characters who compete or imitate Avery’s style, reflecting social trends and heightening stakes.

---

### 4. **Themes**

- Deepen themes around **authenticity vs. performativity** through dialogue and internal conflict.  
- Explore the **cost of validation:** the loneliness behind popularity.  
- Interweave **friendship dynamics** and **personal boundaries** as Avery grapples with concessions she made.  
- Symbolically show the fading camera magic to echo Avery’s faltering confidence.

---

### 5. **Pacing & Structure**

- Use rising tension with intermittent “breathing room” scenes—moments of introspection, sketches, and meaningful talks with Lila.  
- Insert twists: e.g., an unexpected failure of the camera magic, Lucas distancing himself without clear reasons at first, or a social betrayal.  
- Lead to an emotional crescendo: a public confrontation or breakdown that is raw and messy (e.g., a school assembly, party, or viral incident).  
- End Act II on a cliffhanger of uncertainty about Avery’s future and identity.

---

### 6. **Foreshadowing & Payoff**

- Reinforce earlier foreshadowing: camera’s instability evolves into full failure/flashes at critical moments.  
- Lila’s warnings and confrontations pay off emotionally, enriching climax tension.  
- Build up Lucas’s growing frustration into a pivotal emotional choice.  
- Ratchet up Avery’s self-doubt into the breaking point with rising stakes.

---

# **PART III: THE RESOLUTION** (~80,000 - 110,800 words)

### Key Milestones & Plot Points:
- Avery experiences the fading of the camera’s magic—emotional and literal loss of the “main character energy.”  
- Embarks on a cathartic inward journey: revisits art, her sketchbook, quiet places, and relationships.  
- Avery and Lila’s friendship deepens through honest, vulnerable conversations and renewed trust.  
- Lucas appears with a more mature, sincere approach, opening space for honest connection beyond image.  
- Avery embraces self-acceptance and acknowledges her worth independent of external affirmation.  
- Final symbolical scene: scrapped or disabled camera contrasted with revived artistic expression.  
- Closing notes of hopeful ambiguity: Avery stepping confidently into the future, imperfect but authentic.

### Approximate Word Count: 30,800

---

### 1. **Character Development (Avery)**

- Focus on **integration and healing:** Avery reconciles fragmented selves.  
- Use quiet, emotionally charged moments: internal monologues, art sessions, heartfelt conversations.  
- Key decisions: embracing vulnerability publicly, setting boundaries, and rejecting performative popularity.  
- Final growth: personal ownership of identity, redefining ‘main character energy’ on authentic terms.

---

### 2. **Conflict & Tension**

- Tension shifts from external to internal and relational.  
- Include subtle external conflict with Lucas and Lila that resolves through empathy and understanding, not drama.  
- Balance reflective pacing with moments of bittersweet closure (e.g., saying goodbye to the camera, confronting fears).

---

### 3. **Secondary Characters**

- **Lila:** Evolves into an empathetic, supportive presence. Show her own subtle growth—perhaps allowing vulnerability alongside strength.  
- **Lucas:** Moves from surface-level charm to deeper partner: honest conversations reveal emotional depth.  
- Secondary characters fade as Avery finds grounding in herself, but their transformations underline thematic resolutions.

---

### 4. **Themes**

- Celebrate **authenticity, vulnerability, and self-acceptance**.  
- Emphasize **friendship’s endurance and complexity.**  
- Use the **camera as metaphor:** its fading magic echoes Avery’s growth from external validation to internal confidence.  
- Weave hopeful tone; acknowledge imperfection and ongoing journey.

---

### 5. **Pacing & Structure**

- Use slower, reflective pacing overall.  
- Intermingle emotional resolve scenes with quiet personal growth.  
- Include a final twist or reveal if appropriate (e.g., a new creative opportunity, a symbolic gesture with the sketchbook).  
- End with emotionally satisfying yet open-ended closure, suggesting future growth.

---

### 6. **Foreshadowing & Payoff**

- Pay off the camera’s symbolic arc: from magical empowerment to catalyst for growth rather than crutch.  
- Resolve the tension from Lila’s confrontations into strengthened, enduring friendship.  
- Reconcile Lucas’s arc with Avery’s needs for authenticity.  
- Deliver a final thematic payoff in dialogue or internal reflection affirming real “main character energy” is self-defined.

---

# **Additional Tactical Notes**

- **Dialogue tone:** Reflect character profiles consistently—Avery’s poetic introspection vs. Lila’s bluntness and Lucas’s casual charm to enhance realism and voice.  
- **Chapter structure:** Consider alternating Avery’s POV chapters with short moments through Lila or Lucas’s perspective to deepen emotional resonance.  
- **Symbolism reprise:** Periodically return to the sketchbook and camera images to create cohesion.  
- **Scene design:** Use sensory-rich descriptions—Pacific Northwest setting can mirror Avery’s mood shifts (weather changes, shadow/light contrasts).  
- **Cliffhangers:** Strategic placement at Act I and II ends to pull readers forward; Act III focuses on resolution but allows emotional ‘what if’ questions for engagement beyond the last page.
