## PART 1: Shadows of the Past  
*~37,800 words*

### Goal:
Introduce Emery, her grief, and the catalyst for her supernatural ability. Establish the coastal town’s atmosphere and key relationships. Begin hinting at the burden and mystery of the ghosts.

### Key Milestones & Plot Points:  
- **Opening Hook:** Emery’s mother’s death aftermath—evoke raw grief and isolation vividly.  
- **Inciting Incident:** Emery discovers she can see ghosts; the first appearance(s) of the Wistful Woman and other spirits that mirror aspects of her grief.  
- **Introduction of Jonah:** Their initial meeting, tinged with cautious sarcasm and mutual understanding of loss.  
- **First Attempts at Helping Ghosts:** Emery’s initial failures and emotional overwhelm, introducing tension between desire to help and self-protection.  
- **Revelation of the Lighthouse’s Symbolism:** First hints of the lighthouse as a liminal space holding significance for both ghosts and Emery’s family history.  
- **End of Part 1 Cliffhanger:** A particularly unsettling ghost’s arrival or an ominous message hinting at deeper trauma (introduction of the Stubborn Ghost’s shadow).

### Emotional Arc:  
Emery moves from numbness and isolation to tentative curiosity and conflicted responsibility. Her world shifts from a focus on silent grief to forced engagement with pain’s external manifestations.

### Character Development:  
- Emery: Reveal her vulnerability and guilt, plus flashes of empathy fueling her courage.  
- Jonah: Introduce his sarcasm as a coping mechanism, hinting at deeper wounds aligning with Emery’s.  
- Ghosts (Wistful Woman, Sailor): Introduced distinctly with motifs that parallel Emery’s emotional state (regret, duty, longing).

### Conflict & Tension:  
- Internal: Emery battles grief and the yawning loneliness exacerbated by her secret ability.  
- External: Struggle to understand ghosts and their messages amid real-world pressures (school, social expectations).  
- Narrative Balance: Counterbalance emotionally intense ghost encounters with quieter moments of human connection (Emery + Jonah conversations, reflective solitude).  

### Secondary Characters Focus:  
- Jonah’s guarded warmth contrasts with Emery’s openness.  
- The Wistful Woman serves as a metaphorical early mirror for Emery’s own heartbreak and reluctance to move on.  
- The Sailor introduces the theme of duty and unresolved past, enriching the lighthouse symbolism.

### Thematic Infusion:  
- Begin weaving themes of **grief, memory, and the liminal space between holding on and letting go** via dialogue and ghost stories.  
- Use lighthouse as symbolic anchor—light in darkness, boundary between known and unknown.  
- Emery’s locket as a recurring symbol of connection and pain.

### Pacing & Structure:  
- Steady emotional buildup—layer mystery with Emery’s internal processing.  
- End with a minor but compelling cliffhanger tied to the Stubborn Ghost or a memory fragment about her mother.  
- Insert reflective pauses post-ghost encounters to allow reader and Emery to absorb emotional weight.

### Foreshadowing:  
- Seed enigmatic references to Emery’s mother’s final moments interspersed with the ghosts’ narratives.  
- Subtle suggestion of the Stubborn Ghost’s presence—the shadow in peripheral scenes or unexplained feelings of dread.

## PART 2: Into the Mist  
*~38,100 words*

### Goal:  
Deepen emotional complexity and stakes—Emery embraces her role as a guide but faces heightened internal and external challenges. Jonah’s backstory unfolds, paralleling and enriching Emery’s growth. The town’s hidden histories entwine with the supernatural. The core conflict (Stubborn Ghost) intensifies.

### Key Milestones & Plot Points:  
- **Strengthening Bonds:** Developing Emery-Jonah relationship with vulnerable moments and shared pain; small victories and setbacks in trust.  
- **Ghost Interventions:** Emery assisting ghosts through transformational journeys (particularly the Wistful Woman and Sailor), highlighting hope and release.  
- **Emerging Truths:** Discovery of clues about her mother’s past that shake Emery’s foundational beliefs—introduce family secrets or misunderstood events surrounding the accident.  
- **Tensions Rise:** The Stubborn Ghost manifests more aggressively—emotional and possibly supernatural attacks that isolate Emery or risk exposing her secret.  
- **Community Dynamics:** Subtle tensions from townspeople sensing something is amiss; perhaps a skeptical friend or adult challenging Emery’s actions/changes.  
- **Midpoint/New Commitment:** Emery resolves to confront her ghosts more deeply—both spectral and internal—and begins actively seeking reconciliation.  
- **End of Part 2 Cliffhanger:** Confrontation or crisis precipitated by the Stubborn Ghost pushing Emery toward emotional collapse or risking key relationship (Jonah or another ally).

### Emotional Arc:  
From fragile engagement to courageous commitment, Emery confronts deeper fears & guilt. Jonah evolves from sarcasm to genuine emotional support, showcasing his own vulnerability.

### Character Development:  
- Emery: Increasing resilience yet still wrestling with inner turmoil. Takes decisive action to help others and herself.  
- Jonah: Reveals his past losses; learns to trust and opens up meaningfully to Emery.  
- Wistful Woman & Sailor: Reach resolution (release or transformation), reinforcing themes of closure.  
- Stubborn Ghost: Develops into a psychological antagonist, embodying the core emotional obstacle Emery must face.

### Conflict & Tension:  
- Ember rising opposition from stubborn ghost parallels mounting emotional stakes; sustainable pace maintained by interspersing ghost resolutions with reflective quieter scenes.  
- Human relationships strained and tested under the weight of supernatural strain, especially with Jonah.  
- Consider moments of calm domesticity or friendship to prevent burnout and maintain emotional realism.

### Secondary Characters Focus:  
- Jonah increasingly integral—not just sidekick but emotional equal.  
- Townspeople as background characters to hint at the community’s collective shadow or reluctance to confront truths.  
- Ghosts less numerous but more emotionally complex and tied to the overarching narrative.

### Thematic Infusion:  
- Explore **forgiveness, shared pain, and the duality of holding on versus letting go** more poignantly through actions and conversations.  
- Use the lighthouse and fog as metaphors for clarity amidst confusion and transition.

### Pacing & Structure:  
- Narrative intensity peaks mid-part with critical revelations and confrontations.  
- Employ tension breaks with tender moments—e.g., quiet talks on the shoreline, reflective monologues.  
- Build steadily toward climax-related crisis, ending part with emotional and narrative stakes at their highest.

### Foreshadowing & Payoff:  
- Introduce subtle references to the accident’s true nature via flashbacks, symbolism (broken watch, fragments of memory), or ghost hints.  
- Early successes with other ghosts pay off to emphasize Emery’s growth and empower her facing final conflict.

## PART 3: The Light Beyond the Shadows  
*~37,600 words*

### Goal:  
Culminate Emery’s emotional and supernatural journey through confrontation and catharsis. Resolve key relationships and thematic arcs. Deliver satisfying closure, balancing grief’s complexity with hope and acceptance.

### Key Milestones & Plot Points:  
- **Final Ghost Encounters:** Emery’s ultimate confrontation with the Stubborn Ghost—revelation of hidden truths tied to her mother’s death and Emery’s own role in healing.  
- **Emotional Reckoning:** Internal breakthrough scene where Emery accepts the mosaic nature of grief—embracing both pain and love.  
- **Jonah & Emery’s Relationship:** Solidifying connection through shared vulnerability and envisioning a future beyond grief—possibly a quiet, intimate moment symbolizing mutual trust.  
- **Lighthouse Climax:** Symbolic scene around or within the lighthouse representing emotional illumination and boundary crossing from inner darkness to hope.  
- **Ghosts’ Release:** Final spirits find peace, including those connected with the past and the community’s collective history.  
- **Resolution:** Emery steps forward into a tentative new normal, carrying her scars with courage. The lighthouse beam’s glow symbolizes ongoing guidance and possibility.  
- **Closing Image:** Emery and Jonah looking toward a fog-lifted horizon—a visual metaphor for healing and new beginnings.

### Emotional Arc:  
Emery transforms from a vessel of pain to an empowered individual who embraces complexity of loss. Her acceptance allows her to treasure memories without being imprisoned by them.

### Character Development:  
- Emery: Achieves self-acceptance, courage, and emotional release. Acts with clarity and compassion.  
- Jonah: Full partner in healing process, emotionally available and supportive.  
- Stubborn Ghost: Moves from antagonist to symbol of unleashed pain and final release.  
- Supporting ghosts serve as emotional benchmarks for Emery’s progress.

### Conflict & Tension:  
- Highest emotional tension at confrontation with Stubborn Ghost.  
- Softer moments after climax enable reader breathing spaces and reflection.  
- Narrative tension resolves gradually, revealing peace without artificial neatness—remaining true to grief’s complexity.

### Secondary Characters Focus:  
- Jonah as anchor and mirror for Emery’s growth.  
- Remaining ghosts help emphasize final thematic statements—each release a microcosm of letting go.

### Thematic Infusion:  
- Deeply entwine **courage, forgiveness, and the balance of memory and moving forward** in poignant dialogue and symbolism.  
- Use lighthouse beam as metaphor for hope and ongoing journey, not destination.

### Pacing & Structure:  
- Begin part at high tension, peak early with confrontation, then ease into resolution.  
- Use reflective pauses—quiet moments by sea or lighthouse—to underscore transformation.  
- End with hopeful but nuanced closure, avoiding fairy-tale neatness, favoring authentic emotional resonance.

### Foreshadowing & Payoff:  
- Earlier hints about mother’s death, guilt, and lingering spirits find full explanation and emotional resolution.  
- Symbolic objects (locket, broken watch) reappear meaningfully.  
- Jonah’s bracelet and their shared moments symbolize mutual healing and renewal.

# Additional Tactical Guidance Across All Parts:

### Character Development Tips:  
- Use Emery’s internal monologues and dialogue styles to reveal layered emotion—alternate dry wit with heart-on-sleeve moments.  
- Show transformations via key decisions: e.g., choosing to help a ghost despite personal cost, opening up to Jonah, standing up to internal fears.  
- For all ghosts, tie their mini-arcs symbolically and emotionally to thematic motifs and Emery’s growth.

### Conflict & Tension Tips:  
- Structure each ghost encounter to raise stakes or reveal something new about Emery's internal state.  
- Balance intense supernatural sequences with quiet, observational scenes to avoid reader fatigue.  
- Let Jonah’s subtle shifts in tone signal progression from aloofness to tenderness, increasing emotional stakes.  

### Thematic Integration Tips:  
- Use recurring symbols (lighthouse, locket) consistently but with evolving meaning—start as reminders of loss, end as beacons of hope.  
- Weave grief and healing themes into naturalistic dialogue, avoiding preachiness; let characters’ actions embody themes.  
- Employ weather and landscape (fog, storms, dawn light) to metaphorically punctuate emotional beats.

### Pacing & Structure Tips:  
- Pepper chapters’ ends with resonant mini-cliffhangers or reflective questions to sustain curiosity.  
- Vary sentence length and rhythm in ghost scenes to evoke ethereal mood, contrasting with grounded human-world passages.  
- Use shifting POV focus subtly if helpful (primarily through Emery but occasional deep POV glimpses of Jonah or key ghosts to build empathy).

### Foreshadowing & Payoff Tips:  
- Drop subtle sensory details early (e.g., smell or sound linked to mother’s last moments) that make later revelations richer.  
- Introduce symbolic objects early and revisit them with emotional weight to reward attentive readers.  
- Plan your final scene to parallel or mirror a moment from the start, highlighting transformation and thematic closure.
