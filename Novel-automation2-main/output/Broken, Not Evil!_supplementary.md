# Supplementary Materials: Broken, Not Evil!

## Premises & Endings

### Chosen Premise

In a small town steeped in superstition, Mia, a teenager labeled a witch due to her eccentric habits and recent trouble with the law, must confront the lies about her family past and find solace in herself and others who are a misfit like her.

### All Premises

1. In a small town steeped in superstition, Mia, a teenager labeled a witch due to her eccentric habits and recent trouble with the law, must confront the lies about her family past and find solace in herself and others who are misfit like her.

2. After being expelled from multiple schools, Jess is sent to a reform school where her explosive temper is no longer seen as a battle to overcome, but rather a mystery to unravel one layer at a time.

3. Deemed 'trouble' by her uptight suburban community, Eliza uncovers secrets about her own traumatic past that explain her mother's elusive history of mental illness, navigating a new understanding of generational pain.

4. Sam, a rebellious graffiti artist, must realize that society's perception of his family as delinquent is not his burden to bear and that his art can communicate unspoken truths to those willing to see beyond the facade.

5. After being scapegoated for a prank gone wrong, Ava is forced into a community service role where she learns to channel her frustrations into activism, prompting her to question the difference between causing trouble and making change.

6. Expelled for speaking out against school bullies, Raina seeks refuge in an online support group where she learns that embracing her memories with courage helps her escape the shadows of her past.

7. In an oppressive coastal town, Becka finds that the key to healing lies in understanding mysterious truths about her absent father while connecting with an outcast group that reshapes her worldview.

8. Ruby, deemed a notorious liar, finds a confidant in the unlikely form of her elderly neighbor, who helps her sift fact from fiction in a story echoing through generations of their family's legacy.

9. After an unfortunate incident, high school outcast Poppy is mandated to attend a leadership camp, where she discovers her potential as a misunderstood leader who challenges the stereotypes assigned to her.

10. Isolated by rumors after a video of her acting out goes viral, Nora must learn to forgive herself and her tormentors, understanding that her journey is about internal strength, not external acceptance.


### Chosen Ending

Mia exposes a hidden message left by her ancestors within the town's historic records, leading to an awakening within the town and the acknowledgment of past wrongs through restorative justice.

### All Potential Endings

1. Mia discovers her grandmother's hidden journals, learning that her family's history as healers was twisted into tales of witchcraft by the town's founding fathers. She decides to embrace her family's true legacy as a healer.

2. As tensions with the townsfolk reach a breaking point, Mia organizes a community event that encourages storytelling and empathy, gradually dissolving long-held prejudices.

3. Mia's friendship with a fellow outcast leads her to realize that finding a family doesn't always mean correcting the past, but rather choosing the people who support your future.

4. Through a school project about local myths, Mia gains unexpected allies among her peers, who come to see her not as a witch but as a misunderstood figure forced into a narrative beyond her control.

5. Mia's confrontations with her past lead her to a reconciliation with her estranged mother, who reveals the truth about their family's role in the town — not as villains but as defenders against ignorance.

6. Mia exposes a hidden message left by her ancestors within the town's historic records, leading to an awakening within the town and the acknowledgment of past wrongs through restorative justice.

7. Effective by a tragedy close to home, Mia channels her grief into advocating for mental health awareness, helping her peers and the community reassess the labels they've attached to her.

8. Mia inherits a piece of land outside the town bequeathed to her by her estranged grandmother, choosing to create a sanctuary for others like her seeking acceptance.

9. Facing a truth she didn't anticipate, Mia learns her own biases and grows to appreciate the importance of listening, extending understanding beyond her own circle.

10. As Mia packs to leave town for a new start, the townsfolk gift her a book of their collective stories, showing they have finally seen her for who she truly is.


## Novel Plan

To craft a compelling and emotionally rich narrative for your novel "Broken, Not Evil!", follow this structured plan that divides your story into three acts, balancing emotional arcs, conflict escalation, character development, and thematic exploration. Each section is aligned with the arc of your synopsis, ensuring a seamless progression.

### **Act I: The Troublemaker’s Legacy**

**Word Count: 35,000**

#### **1. Character Development:**
- **Zara Lawson:** Establish Zara's foundation as a misunderstood misfit, highlighting her sharp wit and vulnerabilities. Show her isolation and moments of solitude for introspection.
  - **Key Milestone:** Zara discovers the journals, beginning her internal transformation, curious yet fearful of what she may discover.

#### **2. Conflict and Tension:**
- Central Conflict: Zara vs. the community’s perception.
  - **Escalation:** Initial misunderstanding at school and conversations that paint Zara negatively. Balance with quiet moments of Zara reflecting on her struggles and curiosity about the journals.

#### **3. Secondary Characters:**
- **Mia Chen:** Introduce as Zara’s increasingly close friend, offering emotional insight and quiet support.
- **Caleb Turner:** Highlight initial tensions and slow build of camaraderie with Zara based on shared outsider status.
  - **Contribution:** Both characters motivate Zara's journey by providing support and alternative perspectives.

#### **4. Themes:**
- Identity and Perception: Use Zara’s experiences to explore how others’ perceptions shape our identity.
- Rebellion vs. Misunderstanding: Illustrate through Zara’s actions and the town's reactions.

#### **5. Pacing and Structure:**
- Begin with Zara’s daily life, escalate with conflict incidents and the discovery of the journals.
- **Key Scene:** A family confrontation or town incident reveals Zara’s emotional response and desire for truth.
  - Use cliffhangers when Zara learns shocking details about her family’s past.

#### **6. Foreshadowing and Payoff:**
- Introduce subtle hints about Elderspring's history and Zara’s family role in an opening scene, alluding to larger secrets that will unfold.

---

### **Act II: The Maze of Shadows**

**Word Count: 50,000**

#### **1. Character Development:**
- **Zara Lawson:** Deepen Zara’s complexity as she struggles with growing ostracization and a burgeoning sense of identity beyond misfit labels.
  - **Key Moment:** Zara, buoyed by her ancestors’ misunderstood acts, becomes determined to rewrite her story and legacy.

#### **2. Conflict and Tension:**
- Heighten tensions as Zara’s quest risks community backlash. Balance with scenes where Zara and friends bond and strategize.
  - **Midpoint Crisis:** Zara feels the full brunt of peer and societal pressures, yet resolves to push on with allies’ support.

#### **3. Secondary Characters:**
- **Mia Chen:** Develop her influence as she utilizes her artistic skills to help unify and rally the group.
- **Caleb Turner:** Show evolution of his trust and vulnerability, especially in challenging situations.
  - **Role:** Both characters evolve from being allies to critical partners in seeking justice.

#### **4. Themes:**
- Legacy and Redemption: Use journal discoveries and group efforts to explore how understanding the past influences the present.
- Community and Isolation: The growing support from Mia and Caleb contrasts with Zara’s otherwise isolating experiences.

#### **5. Pacing and Structure:**
- Use setbacks (e.g., heightened bullying) and breakthroughs (discovering incriminating records) to present peaks and valleys.
- **Key Scene:** Uncovering a key historical document reveals shocking truths about Elderspring’s fabricated story.

#### **6. Foreshadowing and Payoff:**
- Continue to seed clues from the past that suggest Zara’s journey will reveal transformative truths impacting the whole town.

---

### **Act III: Revealing the Web**

**Word Count: 31,647**

#### **1. Character Development:**
- **Zara Lawson:** Solidify Zara’s growth from an outsider to a figure of change and leadership.
  - **Key Decision:** Leading the town meeting, risking all to unveil the uncomfortable truths.

#### **2. Conflict and Tension:**
- Climax: Zara’s trials peak at the public revelation of the town’s history.
- Emotional Resolution: Show the impact on Zara, her acceptance, and the community’s varied reactions.

#### **3. Secondary Characters:**
- **Mia Chen & Caleb Turner:** Show their journeys from secondary players to integral, supportive characters who contribute significantly to the resolution.
  - **Impact:** Their success in transforming personal struggles into empowering action.

#### **4. Themes:**
- Truth and Justice: Conclude thematic arcs by showing differing responses to uncovered truths.
- Acceptance: Highlight changes in how Zara and her town perceive themselves and each other.

#### **5. Pacing and Structure:**
- Build through rising actions leading to the dramatic town meeting.
- **Resolution Scene:** Acknowledgments and new alliances formed, scene contrasting the early community dynamics with those post-revelation.

#### **6. Foreshadowing and Payoff:**
- Final scenes should tie back to earlier foreshadowing, resolving key mysteries while hinting at continuous community evolution.

By following this structured plan, your novel "Broken, Not Evil!" will achieve a captivating balance of emotional depth, conflict, character development, and thematic resonance, leading readers through a satisfying and thought-provoking narrative journey.
